## 1) Objective
The goal of this work is to evaluate how **Dropout**, **Batch Normalization**, and weight initialization methods (**Default / He / Xavier**) affect:
- convergence speed
- final classification accuracy
- generalization behavior

The experiments specifically analyze the interaction of **Dropout + BatchNorm + initialization** under controlled settings.

## 2) Experimental Setup
### Dataset
- CIFAR-10 (via `torchvision`)

### Training Configuration
- Quick run: 4 epochs
- Extended run: 6 epochs
- Batch size: 256
- Subset ratio: 0.5
- Optimizer/scheduler: baseline project pipeline settings

### Model Configuration
- Dropout enabled in fully connected layer (`dropout_rate=0.5`)
- BatchNorm enabled in convolutional blocks
- Initialization schemes:
  - `default`
  - `he`
  - `xavier`

## 3) Executed Commands

## 4) Experimental Results

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|

| He | 10.12 | 10.12 | 9.90 | ~0.00 |
| Xavier | 49.04 | 36.18 | 49.04 | ~0.00 |

## 5) Technical Observations
- **Default initialization** produced the highest and most stable performance.
- **Xavier** showed consistent learning and improved with more epochs.
- **He** did not converge well in this configuration (Dropout + BN + current hyperparameters).
- BatchNorm improved training stability in Default and Xavier settings.
- Initialization choice had a stronger effect on accuracy than dropout ratio in these runs.

## 6) Convergence Analysis
- Default converged faster and reached stable validation performance earlier.
- Xavier required more epochs but followed a stable upward trend.
- He remained near-stagnant, indicating mismatch with current architecture/training setup.

## 8) Conclusion
This study shows that initialization strategy materially changes convergence and final performance.
Within the tested setup, **Default > Xavier >> He** in terms of practical accuracy and stability.
