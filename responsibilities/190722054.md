5. Key Technical Observations
Default initialization achieved the highest and most stable performance across both runs.
Xavier initialization showed consistent learning behavior, improving steadily with epochs.
He initialization failed to converge properly under this architecture with Dropout + BN combination.
Batch Normalization significantly stabilized training dynamics across Default and Xavier setups.
Dropout contributed to regularization but did not prevent underperformance in poorly initialized (He) setups.
Overall, initialization choice had a stronger impact than Dropout ratio in this experiment.

6. Convergence Analysis
Default model converged faster and reached stable accuracy earlier.
Xavier model required more epochs but showed continuous improvement.
He initialization exhibited near-stagnant training, indicating incompatibility with current hyperparameter configuration.

8. Conclusion

This experiment demonstrates that:

Proper weight initialization is critical for convergence stability.
