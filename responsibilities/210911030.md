## FGSM

\[
x_{\text{adv}} = \mathrm{clip}\bigl(x + \varepsilon \cdot \mathrm{sign}(\nabla_x \mathcal{L})\bigr)
\]

Because inputs are normalized, clipping in `fgsm_eval.py` is applied using per-channel bounds in the normalized space.

### 2) FGSM — optimizer / gradient clipping (full measurement)

| Model | Clean | ε=0.05 | ε=0.1 | ε=0.2 |
|:---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| SGD (No Clip) | 44.30 | 17.35 | 14.72 | 10.59 |
| SGD (Clip=1.0) | 36.11 | 27.39 | 22.24 | 14.97 |
| Adam (No Clip) | 77.18 | 21.02 | 14.89 | 10.42 |
| Adam (Clip=1.0) | **77.75** | 21.14 | 14.51 | 10.33 |

Highest clean accuracy: Adam (Clip=1.0). SGD + clipping can show a relatively smoother drop at some ε values, despite lower clean accuracy.
